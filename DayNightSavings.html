Day/Night Savings
UX Design Prototype
The Task
"Create an application, website, tool, etc. that will allow customers to perform the activities most important to them when seeking to fulfill specific health and wellness needs.‚Äù
The Final Product
Our Final Product was a fully functional prototype of an app that would help users visualise and limit their screentime on multiple devices
Our Process
In my team of three, we followed the UX Design Process in order to understand, imagine, and create the final product
Step 1: Scoping
Using numerous sticky notes, we considered the prompt and brainstormed variousideas around technology and health and wellness needs
We decided to explore device usage and stress/anxiety because of it's prevalence in recent times
Step 2: Research
Taking the previous idea into mind, we gathered data relating to those topics through live interviews and a thorough 6 part Google survey that we distributed to friends and family
Step 3: Analysis
After analyzing both survey and interview results, we were able to find a significant use of digital devices and stress/anxiety.
Step 4: Ideation
Here we created user personas for our target audiences, one of which was the Struggling Student Stacy who had a hard time managing their screen time and studying
After that we ideated the primitive version of Day/Night Savings: "Time Saver."
This would be an app that would help users struggling with excessive device usage by logging their screen time accross multiple devices
Step 5.1: Design Planning
We created a sitemap, user flows, and wireframes to help us create the appropriate architecture for our prototype
Step 5.2: Designing
Using Axure, we created a very primitive prototype of "Time Saver." We used a blue UI to promote a sense of calmness and peace with rounded buttons and fonts to create friendliness
Step 6: Validation and Reiteration
After User Tests, we were able to find parts of our program that we found to be unintuitive. We went batck and redesigned individual parts of the prototype.
After the class ended, I got more critical feedback and decided to rebrand and redesign the entire interface to give it a more modern aesthetic. I used consistent online UI as well as brightly contrasted colors to make the app "pop" more
The Final Product?
Just like writing, there is always something that could be better. I have come to understand that design is a never ending process and through constant re-evaluations I can slowly improve my work to be better than before
Creating a Better User Experience
By converting screen time data into visual charts, users gain instant visual insights into their daily, weekly, or monthly usage across multiple devices. This simplifies comprehension, eliminating the need to interpret raw numbers
